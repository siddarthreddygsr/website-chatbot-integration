Understanding Contracts in the A&D Business Landscape

A&D contracts are of various types, each with its own set of nuances. Fixed-price contracts require strict adherence to budget and timelines. Cost-plus-fee contracts reimburse actual costs incurred with an added fee. Time & material contracts track labor hours and material expenses for billing which can be cumbersome.

Challenges in A&D Government Contracts and Billing
  • Manual Processes: A reliance on manual data entry and repetitive tasks opens the door to errors and slows down workflows.
  • Cost Tracking Challenges: Accurately tracking project costs across different contracts, work breakdown structures (WBS), and deliverables per DCAA and CAS compliance can be a significant hurdle.
  • Compliance Burdens: Navigating strict government contracting regulations, including  DCAA, DCMA, and CAS requirements, across all contracts and billing documents, adds another layer of complexity.
  • Communication Silos: Inconsistent data and lack of visibility across key stakeholders can lead to delays and misunderstandings.
